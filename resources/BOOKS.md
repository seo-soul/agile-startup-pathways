# Recommended Books

A curated reading list for agile practitioners and software engineers. Books are grouped by theme and annotated with who they're most useful for.

---

## Agile & Scrum

**Scrum: The Art of Doing Twice the Work in Half the Time**
*Jeff Sutherland* — One of the co-creators of Scrum explains the framework's origins and core mechanics. Required reading for anyone new to Scrum.

**Agile Software Development: Principles, Patterns, and Practices**
*Robert C. Martin* — Deep dive into agile development with practical examples in Java. Heavy on engineering discipline.

**Extreme Programming Explained: Embrace Change**
*Kent Beck* — The book that defined XP. Covers practices like TDD, pair programming, continuous integration, and short release cycles.

**User Story Mapping**
*Jeff Patton* — How to build shared understanding across a team using story maps. Practical and immediately applicable.

---

## Engineering Excellence

**Clean Code: A Handbook of Agile Software Craftsmanship**
*Robert C. Martin* — Standards for writing readable, maintainable code. Controversial in places but widely influential.

**The Pragmatic Programmer: Your Journey to Mastery**
*David Thomas & Andrew Hunt* — Career-spanning advice for software craftspeople. Covers everything from personal responsibility to tooling.

**Continuous Delivery: Reliable Software Releases through Build, Test, and Deployment Automation**
*Jez Humble & David Farley* — The canonical guide to CI/CD pipelines and deployment practices.

**Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps**
*Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble & Gene Kim* — Research-backed evidence for what high-performing engineering teams actually do differently.

---

## Systems Thinking & Culture

**The Phoenix Project: A Novel about IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win**
*Gene Kim, Kevin Behr & George Spafford* — A novel-format introduction to DevOps thinking. Easy to read, hard to put down.

**Team Topologies: Organizing Business and Technology Teams for Fast Flow**
*Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais* — How to structure teams to minimize cognitive load and optimize for fast delivery.

**The Lean Startup**
*Eric Ries* — Applying lean manufacturing principles to product development. The foundation of build-measure-learn thinking.

---

## Mindset & Motivation

**Drive: The Surprising Truth About What Motivates Us**
*Daniel Pink* — Research on intrinsic motivation: autonomy, mastery, and purpose. Relevant for understanding what makes engineering teams thrive.

**Mindset: The New Psychology of Success**
*Carol S. Dweck* — Growth vs. fixed mindset. Foundational for understanding how individuals and teams improve over time.

---

## Reference

**Software Engineering at Google**
*Titus Winters, Tom Manshreck & Hyrum Wright* — Free online. How Google approaches software engineering at scale. Practical and thoughtful.
