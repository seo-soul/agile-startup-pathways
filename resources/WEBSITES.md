# Recommended Websites

A curated list of online resources for learning and referencing agile practices, engineering standards, and team culture.

---

## Agile Foundations

**agilemanifesto.org**
The original Agile Manifesto site. Read it, bookmark it, revisit it periodically.

**scrumguides.org**
The official Scrum Guide, maintained by Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland. Free PDF download. This is the authoritative definition of Scrum.

**scrum.org**
Professional Scrum certifications (PSM, PSPO, etc.) and a large library of free learning resources, articles, and assessments.

**scrumalliance.org**
CSM and other Scrum Alliance certifications. Also maintains the State of Scrum report published annually.

---

## Engineering Practices

**martinfowler.com**
Martin Fowler's site is an encyclopedic reference for software design, refactoring, architecture, and agile engineering practices. The bliki (blog + wiki) format makes it easy to explore related topics.

**extremeprogramming.org**
Don Wells's site explaining XP practices in plain terms. A good quick-reference for pair programming, TDD, simple design, and continuous integration.

**refactoring.guru**
Visual, accessible explanations of design patterns and refactoring techniques. Great for junior developers.

---

## DevOps & Delivery

**dora.dev**
The DORA (DevOps Research and Assessment) team's site. Tracks the four key metrics: deployment frequency, lead time, MTTR, and change failure rate. Take the annual State of DevOps Report seriously.

**12factor.net**
The Twelve-Factor App methodology for building software-as-a-service. Opinionated, practical, widely adopted.

---

## Community & News

**infoq.com**
News, articles, podcasts, and presentations on software development. Heavy on architecture and engineering leadership topics.

**thoughtworks.com/radar**
The ThoughtWorks Technology Radar — a quarterly opinionated guide to techniques, tools, platforms, and languages worth adopting, trialing, assessing, or avoiding.

**dev.to**
Community-driven developer blog platform. Quality varies but there's excellent content from practitioners across every stack.

**stackoverflow.blog**
Engineering culture, career development, and technical deep-dives from the Stack Overflow team.

---

## Reference Tools

**c2.com/cgi/wiki**
The original Wiki, created by Ward Cunningham. Historical context for agile and XP concepts.

**conventionalcommits.org**
A lightweight convention for structuring commit messages. Worth adopting as a team standard.

**semver.org**
Semantic versioning specification. If you're building versioned software, know this.
