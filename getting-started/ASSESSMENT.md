# Getting Started: Assessment

The first step in your journey is to better understand who you are, and how to communicate that identity to others. We use both standardized personality testing and professional assessments for all staff to help us figure out how to collaborate and communicate effectively.

How much you decide to share is up to you. However, it is important that your assigned mentor has access to your results before your first 1:1.

---

## Why Assessments?

Great teams aren't built from identical people — they're built from complementary ones. These tools help us:

- Understand how each person prefers to receive information and feedback
- Identify potential friction points before they become problems
- Match mentorship and collaboration styles to individual needs
- Have more productive conversations about working styles

These are tools for self-awareness, not evaluation. They don't affect your performance reviews.

---

## Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)

**Purpose:** A broadly recognized framework for understanding communication and processing styles.

The MBTI describes personality across four dimensions:

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **I / E** | Introversion vs. Extraversion — how you recharge |
| **S / N** | Sensing vs. Intuition — how you take in information |
| **T / F** | Thinking vs. Feeling — how you make decisions |
| **J / P** | Judging vs. Perceiving — how you structure your world |

**Take the assessment:** [16personalities.com](https://www.16personalities.com) (free, ~12 minutes)

**Note:** The MBTI is a useful conversational tool and a fun way to learn more about how you process and communicate information. We do not use it to make business or role decisions — personality assessments are inherently variable and context-dependent.

## PrinciplesYou (Big Five / OCEAN)

**Purpose:** Our primary professional assessment. This is the framework we use to inform team composition, mentorship, and collaboration recommendations.

The Big Five (OCEAN) model measures:

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Openness** | Curiosity, creativity, openness to new experiences |
| **Conscientiousness** | Organization, dependability, goal-directed behavior |
| **Extraversion** | Sociability, assertiveness, positive emotionality |
| **Agreeableness** | Cooperation, trust, empathy |
| **Neuroticism** | Emotional volatility, stress reactivity |

**Take the assessment:** [principlesyou.com](https://principlesyou.com) (free with account, ~20 minutes)

**Created by:** Ray Dalio of Bridgewater Associates. The assessment is grounded in decades of academic research on personality psychology.

**Why this one matters more:** Unlike the MBTI, the Big Five is well-supported by peer-reviewed research and shows strong predictive validity for workplace behavior. Your PrinciplesYou report will form the basis of your first in-depth conversation with your mentor.

---

## What to Do With Your Results

1. Complete both assessments before your first mentor 1:1
2. Save your results (PDF or screenshot)
3. Share them with your mentor via your agreed-upon channel
4. Come ready to discuss: what resonates, what surprises you, what you'd push back on

You are more than a four-letter type or a set of percentages. These tools are conversation starters, not conclusions.

---

## Additional Assessments (Optional)

As you grow into the team, you may find these supplementary tools useful:

- **CliftonStrengths (StrengthsFinder)** — Identifies your top natural talents across 34 themes
- **DISC** — A simpler behavioral model often used in sales and leadership contexts
- **Enneagram** — A type-based framework with a focus on core motivations and fears

Ask your mentor if any of these are relevant to your role.
